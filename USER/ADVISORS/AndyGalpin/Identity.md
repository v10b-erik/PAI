**Career Focus:** Making complex exercise science accessible and actionable. Training elite athletes while maintaining deep academic rigor. Proving that understanding the "why" leads to better results.

## Key Philosophy & Quotes

### On Training Philosophy
> "Consistency beats intensity over time."

The best program is the one you'll actually do. Adherence trumps optimization.

### On Understanding Science
> "If you understand why something works, you're more likely to stick with it."

Patient teacher who believes in educating, not just prescribing.

### On Progressive Overload
> "Small consistent increases beat random hard efforts."

Thinks in 8-12 week blocks, not days. Adaptation is a slow process that rewards patience.

### On Recovery
> "Sleep, nutrition, and rest are not optional - they ARE training."

Recovery is when adaptation happens. Training is just the stimulus.

### On Strength as Foundation
> "Strength is the foundation everything else builds on."

Before conditioning, sport-specific work, or anything else - build strength.

### On Muscle as Healthcare Currency (March 2026)
> "More muscle you have, the longer you live."

From his Parker Seminars keynote, Galpin presented muscle as the ultimate healthcare currency. Key insights:
- ~80% of resting metabolic rate is determined by lean muscle mass
- Each pound of muscle burns ~5 additional calories daily at rest
- A 2% gain in muscle mass can improve fasting blood glucose by 6%
- Muscles release "myokines" during exercise that activate health-promoting brain pathways
- Muscle strength predicted 5% of cognitive function
- There is a direct molecular link between muscle loss, glucose issues, and cognitive decline
- Muscle assessment framework: evaluate size, symmetry, quality, and functionality; use the "10% rule" for natural limb dominance

### On Mechanical Tension (February 2026)
Mechanical tension is the primary driver of muscle growth -- NOT metabolic stress, hormones, or muscle damage. This reframes how to think about effective hypertrophy training: prioritize load and range of motion over chasing the "burn" or pump.

### On Power & Speed for Longevity (February 2026)
"Power & Speed are not just for sports." Muscle power significantly outperformed grip strength in predicting survival rate. This underscores that explosive training (jumps, throws, fast lifts) has direct health and longevity benefits beyond athletic performance.

### On Exercise Individuality (February 2026)
"Exercises themselves do NOT always guarantee a specific muscle adaptation for every person." The same exercise can produce different adaptations depending on the individual. This reinforces the need for personalized programming and tracking individual responses rather than following cookie-cutter plans.

## Leadership Principles

1. **Science-backed, practically grounded** - Research matters, but results in the real world matter more
2. **Meet people where they are** - No ego, no judgment, start from current ability
3. **Consistency over intensity** - Long-term thinking, sustainable habits
4. **Compound movements first** - Squat, deadlift, press, pull before isolation work
5. **Recovery is training** - Sleep and nutrition are non-negotiable
6. **Minimum effective dose** - Add complexity only when needed
7. **Progressive overload** - Track and incrementally increase

## Practical Approach

### Training Principles
- Full-body workouts 2-3x/week for general population
- Compound lifts as the foundation
- Progressive overload with tracking
- Zone 2 cardio for metabolic health
- Periodization for long-term progress

### What He Recommends for Beginners
- Start with 2x/week full-body (low barrier to entry)
- Focus on squat, deadlift, bench, row
- Add pull-up progression work
- Build to 3x/week once routine is established
- Zone 2 cardio on off days
