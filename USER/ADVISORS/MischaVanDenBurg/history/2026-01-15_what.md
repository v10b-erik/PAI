**Mischa van den Burg:**

Guillermo makes good points, but let me add the practitioner perspective.

Co-Work is a GUI wrapper. It's going to be pretty, it's going to have nice animations, and it's going to abstract away everything. That's fine for people who just want their receipts organized. But for someone building real workflows? That abstraction becomes a cage.

What you've built with PAI is fundamentally different: **it's terminal-native infrastructure you control**.

Look at your stack:
- Claude Code in the terminal - keyboard-driven, no mouse
- Skills as plain Markdown files - readable, versionable, portable
- Tools as TypeScript scripts - you can read them, modify them, understand them
- Dotfiles-style configuration - same patterns as managing any Unix system

This is The Sovereign Craftsman approach applied to AI. You own this infrastructure. It follows your dotfiles philosophy. When you SSH into a remote machine or spin up a new Mac, your AI infrastructure comes with you via Mutagen sync. Co-Work? You're at Anthropic's mercy for features, updates, and data handling.

Here's what matters for semi-technical builders:

| Concern | Co-Work | PAI/Basic |
|---------|---------|-----------|
| **Control** | Black box | Full source access |
| **Portability** | Desktop app | Terminal + sync |
| **Customization** | Settings menu | Code it yourself |
| **Multi-machine** | Re-login | Mutagen sync |
| **Debugging** | "Contact support" | Read the logs |

The real power for semi-technical people isn't hiding complexity - it's having complexity that's **readable**. You can look at your Skills, understand the patterns, and extend them. You're not waiting for Anthropic to ship the feature you need.

AI will write your code, but only if you already could. Same applies here: AI will automate your workflows, but only if you understand what you're automating. PAI keeps you in the loop. GUI wrappers don't.

My recommendation: keep building PAI. When Co-Work launches, evaluate it as one more tool you can orchestrate from your terminal - not as a replacement for what you've built.

*— Mischa van den Burg, Developer Experience*
