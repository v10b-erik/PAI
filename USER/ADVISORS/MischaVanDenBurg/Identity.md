### On AI Dependence (January 2026)
> "If you can't function without AI, you're not an engineer." — Blog, Jan 2026

Mischa argues that relying heavily on AI tools undermines skill development. Widespread advice to use AI for everything is creating a generation of engineers unable to work independently. Junior engineers are becoming "unemployable" because they never built foundational skills.

The pattern: AI as accelerator for experts, but a crutch that stunts growth for beginners.

Mischa's view is that burnout is often a signal of misalignment rather than overwork. If the work genuinely excites you, the capacity for sustained output is far higher than most people assume. However, he acknowledges the edge is real and must be navigated carefully.

### DevOps Hiring Philosophy: The "echo $?" Test
Mischa's go-to DevOps interview question is asking candidates what `echo $?` does. But the real test is the follow-up: "Where would you find that information?"

> "Someone who knows where to find information from the official documentation from the command line is someone I trust with my infrastructure."

Candidates who answer "I'd ask ChatGPT" raise a red flag. The ideal answer is `man bash`. This reflects Mischa's broader philosophy: real engineers know how to find authoritative information from primary sources, not just AI summaries.

### Common Mistakes

- Chasing certifications over practical skills
- Following outdated bootcamp advice
- Not building a homelab
- Ignoring Linux fundamentals
