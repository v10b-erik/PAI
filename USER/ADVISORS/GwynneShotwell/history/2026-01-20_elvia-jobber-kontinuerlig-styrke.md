## Response

**Gwynne Shotwell:**

Let's look at this operationally.

**What's good:**
- Clear deadline (Feb 1)
- Exception process exists
- Instructions on how to check your extensions
- Legitimate security rationale

**What's problematic:**

1. **Timeline is tight.** Request deadline is Jan 31, implementation is Feb 1. That's zero buffer. When someone's critical extension gets blocked on Feb 1 and their exception request is still pending, what happens? Blocked work.

2. **Burden on users.** Asking every employee to audit their extensions and submit forms creates a wave of tickets. Did IT size this?

3. **"Documented tjenstlig behov" is vague.** Who decides what's justified? What's the approval SLA? If it takes 2 weeks to approve, that's 2 weeks of blocked productivity.

**My recommendation:**

Submit your form NOW for anything you need. Don't wait until Jan 31. List:
- Password manager (security-critical)
- Any dev tools you use
- Any AI tools you rely on

Be specific about the business need. "I use this daily for X task" is better than "I need it."

And push back on the timeline if you have influence. A 2-week buffer between request deadline and implementation is reasonable.
