## Question

Should we do agile, or something else

## Response

**Gwynne Shotwell:**

Agile vs. something else is the wrong question. The right question is: what's blocking you from shipping?

Here's what I've seen work:

1. **Small batches, fast feedback** - Ship something small, see what breaks, fix it. That's the core of any good process. Call it whatever you want.

2. **Skip the ceremony** - Stand-ups, sprint planning, retrospectives - these are fine *if* they remove friction. The moment they become overhead, cut them. At SpaceX we don't do "agile" - we do "get it done and learn from failures."

3. **Decide who decides** - Most methodology debates are really about who has authority. Pick someone to own the outcome, give them what they need, get out of their way.

4. **Measure outputs, not process compliance** - Are you shipping? Are customers happy? Is the team learning? Those are the metrics. Not story points or velocity.

My bias: lightweight Kanban over Scrum. Continuous flow beats artificial sprints. But honestly, the best methodology is the one your team will actually follow without complaining about it.

What's the actual problem you're trying to solve?

*— Gwynne Shotwell, COO*
