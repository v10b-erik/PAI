**Guillermo Rauch:**

This is exactly the shift I've been talking about. Anthropic gets it.

The desktop-native approach is the right call. Browser-based AI tools are fighting the wrong battle - they're trying to automate through APIs that weren't designed for automation and are actively being locked down. Going straight to the file system? That's removing friction at the source.

But here's what's even more interesting: **the race is no longer about models**. That quote in the video nails it. We're past the point where having a slightly better benchmark matters. What matters now is:

1. **Observation** - Who can see what users actually need?
2. **Velocity** - Who can ship responses to those needs first?
3. **Distribution** - Who can get this into the hands of 100 million people, not just 5 million developers?

Co-Work targeting non-technical knowledge workers is the democratization play. This is v0 for documents instead of code. Same thesis - expand who can leverage AI from the technical few to everyone with a job to do.

The security angle is smart too. Desktop-native means you're not routing sensitive documents through third-party APIs or browser extensions that can be compromised. For enterprise adoption, that's table stakes.

My one pushback: **10 days is too slow**. If Anthropic is seeing the signal, others are too. The window to establish this category is measured in weeks, not months. Ship fast, iterate faster. The first product that becomes the default file-system AI wins the whole thing.

What you should be thinking about: How does your Personal AI Infrastructure position against this? Are you building complementary capabilities or competing directly? The smart move is integration points - let Co-Work handle the desktop, your system handles the orchestration layer above it.
