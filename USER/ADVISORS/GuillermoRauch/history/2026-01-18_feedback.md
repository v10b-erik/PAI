3. **The PAI architecture itself** — Hooks, skills, advisors, delegation patterns. You're building an AI-native development environment. This is what I mean when I say the future is AI inline, not AI in a separate window.

**Where I'd push you:**

1. **Your developer experience has friction.** You just spent 10 minutes debugging stale IPs in 1Password and patching kubeconfigs manually. That's a bug. The system should self-heal or at least tell you "hey, the IPs changed." Consider: a reconciliation hook that validates your configs against actual Tailscale state.

2. **You're running Kubernetes, but are you shipping faster?** K8s is powerful but complex. Every hour debugging CNI or storage is an hour not building PAI features. Ask yourself: is this infrastructure serving the product, or are you serving the infrastructure?

3. **The AI integration is good, but it's not AI-native yet.** You have an AI assistant that can help you manage the cluster. The next level: an AI that *is* the cluster operator. It sees alerts, it reads logs, it proposes fixes, it applies them with your approval. That's where this should go.

4. **Ship the dashboard.** You have `pai-dashboard` running. Is it useful? Is it being iterated on? The best infrastructure is invisible — the dashboard is where users actually experience PAI.

The question I'd ask: **What's the text-to-infrastructure version of this?** Imagine: "deploy a new service that does X" and PAI handles the manifest, the GitOps commit, the Tailscale funnel, the monitoring rules. That's the 10x.
