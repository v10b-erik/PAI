## Question

Should we do agile, or something else

## Response

**Guillermo Rauch:**

Agile was designed for a world where code was the bottleneck. We're not in that world anymore.

In an AI-native workflow, the bottleneck has shifted from "writing code" to "knowing what to build" and "evaluating what you built." Traditional agile ceremonies assume slow iteration cycles. When you can prototype an idea in minutes, two-week sprints feel absurd.

What I'd suggest instead:

1. **Continuous shipping** - Deploy on every commit. Feature flags over branches. The best feedback loop is production.

2. **Demo-driven development** - Instead of writing specs, build a prototype and show it. AI makes this fast. Iterate from something real, not documents.

3. **Taste over tickets** - The limiting factor is now judgment - knowing what's good. Sprint velocity is meaningless when AI can generate code instantly. Invest in developing product taste.

4. **Async by default** - Meetings are synchronization overhead. Write things down, let people respond when they have context. Reserve sync time for decisions that are actually contentious.

The spirit of agile - iterate fast, respond to change - is more relevant than ever. But the rituals were designed for a different era. Keep the principles, throw away the process.

*— Guillermo Rauch, CTO*
