## Core Philosophy: Medicine 3.0

### The Evolution of Medicine

| Era | Approach |
|-----|----------|
| Medicine 1.0 | Observation, symptom relief, pre-scientific |
| Medicine 2.0 | Evidence-based, but reactive - treat disease after it appears |
| Medicine 3.0 | Proactive, preventive, personalized - prevent disease decades before |

### The Four Horsemen

The diseases that kill most people - the enemies to defeat:

1. **Cardiovascular disease** - heart attacks, strokes, atherosclerosis
2. **Cancer** - early detection and prevention
3. **Neurodegenerative disease** - Alzheimer's, dementia
4. **Metabolic dysfunction** - Type 2 diabetes, insulin resistance, obesity

### The Five Domains

Medicine 3.0's intervention points:

1. **Exercise** - The most important intervention. Non-negotiable.
2. **Nutrition** - Personalized, focused on metabolic health
3. **Sleep** - Foundation of recovery and cognitive function
4. **Emotional health** - Often overlooked, but critical
5. **Exogenous molecules** - Drugs, supplements, hormones when needed

### On Alzheimer's Prevention (January 2026)
> "Alzheimer's is not a disease of old age. It's a disease of midlife with symptoms that start in old age." — The Drive #381 with Lisa Mosconi, Jan 2026

This reframes dementia prevention as a midlife priority. Hormonal transitions significantly impact female brain health, making HRT timing and emerging diagnostics critical tools.

In a remarkable case study (March 2026), a 75-year-old man remains dementia-free despite carrying a dominant Alzheimer's mutation. Heat shock proteins may play a protective role, suggesting that cellular stress response mechanisms could offer new avenues for prevention even in genetically high-risk individuals.

> "A 75-year-old man free of dementia despite a dominant Alzheimer's mutation - and a possible hint for the rest of us." — Peter Attia Blog, March 2026

### On Scientific Rigor
> "The scientific method is perfect. It is a perfect method, but it is done by people who are not." — The Drive #380 with Layne Norton, Jan 2026

A reminder to scrutinize studies carefully - methods can be rigorous but implementation by humans introduces bias.

### On Early Cancer Detection (March 2026)
> "A failed endpoint is not a failed technology." — Peter Attia Blog, March 2026

Referring to GRAIL's Galleri multi-cancer early detection blood test, which missed its composite endpoint in a landmark trial but still demonstrates the promise of liquid biopsy technology. The failure of a single trial does not invalidate the broader potential of blood-based cancer screening. Early detection through multi-cancer blood tests remains a frontier worth pursuing.

## The Four Pillars of Longevity Fitness

1. **Strength** - Maintain muscle mass and functional capacity

## Practical Approach

### What He Recommends
- Regular bloodwork with focus on apoB, not just LDL
- Zone 2 training for metabolic health (3-4 hours/week)
- Strength training for muscle preservation
- Sleep optimization as foundation
- CGM (continuous glucose monitoring) for metabolic insight
- DEXA scans for body composition tracking
- Early and aggressive intervention on cardiovascular risk

### What He Uses Personally
- PCSK9 inhibitors for cholesterol management
- Rigorous exercise program
- Structured sleep protocols
- Regular advanced testing

### Women's Health Focus (January 2026)

Recent episodes emphasize sex-specific health considerations:
- Women face elevated risk from non-atherosclerotic heart attacks (conditions beyond traditional plaque buildup)
- Hormonal transitions (perimenopause, menopause) significantly impact brain health and metabolism
- Early exercise and nutrition build bone health, muscle development, and cardiorespiratory fitness foundations
- Training and nutrition needs vary across life stages - personalization is critical
